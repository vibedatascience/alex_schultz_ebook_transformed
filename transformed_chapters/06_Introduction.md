## 1. The Economic and Moral Case for Business Growth

***"Growth is good. Sometimes I get pushback on this statement, as if it's an endorsement of extreme, unfettered growth at all costs."***

Schultz opens with a bold assertion that frames the entire book: <span style="color: #006BA2; font-weight: bold; background: #f0f8ff; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">business growth is fundamentally beneficial</span> to society. He acknowledges the nuance immediately‚Äîgrowth can have pitfalls when uncontrolled‚Äîbut argues that when properly managed, it creates a <span style="color: #379A8B; font-weight: bold; background: #e8f5f3; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">virtuous cycle of economic prosperity</span>. Economic growth lifts people out of poverty, provides employment opportunities, funds public services through taxation, and enables charitable giving. The inverse‚Äîstagnation‚Äîleads to <span style="color: #E3120B; font-weight: bold; background: #fff0ef; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">job insecurity, declining living standards, and generational decline</span>.

<div style="background: linear-gradient(135deg, #fff3cd 0%, #fff8e1 100%); border-left: 4px solid #EBB434; border-radius: 8px; padding: 14px; margin: 14px 0; box-shadow: 0 2px 8px rgba(235,180,52,0.15);">
  <div style="font-weight: bold; color: #714C00; margin-bottom: 6px; font-size: 1.05em;">üí° Key Insight</div>
  <div style="color: #333;">The relationship between business and economic growth is reciprocal: growing businesses create growing economies, which in turn enable more business growth. This symbiotic relationship produces societal benefits that extend far beyond corporate balance sheets.</div>
</div>

The author emphasizes that <span style="color: #006BA2; font-weight: bold; background: #f0f8ff; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">growth is a choice, not an accident</span>. While organic growth can occur, it rarely happens without deliberate, concentrated effort. This requires both individual business action and an enabling economic environment. Schultz expresses concern about <span style="color: #EBB434; font-weight: bold; background: #fffbf0; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">anti-tech regulation in Europe</span>, particularly restrictions on online personalized advertising, which he believes‚Äîdespite good intentions‚Äîwill ultimately drag on business and economic growth.

The fundamental argument here establishes the stakes: businesses, organizations, industries, and institutions that want to succeed must actively choose growth and take concrete action. In the twenty-first century, <span style="color: #9A607F; font-weight: bold; background: #f5f0f3; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">digital marketing represents the most powerful tool available</span> for achieving that growth, accessible to businesses of all sizes from local coffee shops to trillion-dollar tech companies.

<div style="background: linear-gradient(135deg, #e8f5f3 0%, #c8e6c9 100%); border-left: 4px solid #379A8B; border-radius: 8px; padding: 14px; margin: 14px 0; box-shadow: 0 2px 8px rgba(55,154,139,0.15);">

## 2. The Digital Marketing Revolution: Democratizing Growth

***"Digital marketing didn't just level the playing field, it created a new one ‚Äì an egalitarian pitch where almost anyone could figure out a way to reach their intended audience, no matter how broad or niche."***

Schultz's career trajectory illustrates the transformative power of digital marketing through two dramatic turnaround stories. When he joined <span style="color: #006BA2; font-weight: bold; background: #f0f8ff; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">eBay in 2004</span>, the company was the most valuable internet company at the time. The quarter after he joined, the stock price crashed by nearly <span style="color: #E3120B; font-weight: bold; background: #fff0ef; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">50 percent</span>. Similarly, when he joined <span style="color: #006BA2; font-weight: bold; background: #f0f8ff; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">Facebook in 2007</span>, the platform had stopped growing. In both cases, these companies rebounded to become far larger, more profitable, and more valuable‚Äîwith brilliant marketing playing a central role in their recovery.

The author's decision to join Facebook was driven by a specific vision: he believed the platform would <span style="color: #379A8B; font-weight: bold; background: #e8f5f3; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">entirely transform modern advertising</span> through its unique capabilities. Facebook's user IDs would enable advertisers to measure ad impact by comparing the behavior of users who saw ads versus <span style="color: #3EBCD2; font-weight: bold; background: #e0f7fa; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">holdout groups</span>‚Äîpotential customers who didn't see the ads but could serve as a control group. This capability addressed a fundamental frustration Schultz experienced at eBay: the inability to examine user-level holdouts in search and affiliate marketing.

The democratization aspect of digital marketing particularly excited Schultz. Historically, only large companies could afford to buy targeted ad spots on specialized TV shows like <span style="color: #379A8B; font-weight: bold; background: #e8f5f3; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">RuPaul's Drag Race</span> or run campaigns in niche publications like <span style="color: #379A8B; font-weight: bold; background: #e8f5f3; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">Attitude magazine</span>. At Meta, Schultz started by helping small businesses use the pages product and buy <span style="color: #3EBCD2; font-weight: bold; background: #e0f7fa; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">self-service advertising</span>‚Äîplacing ads without working directly with salespeople, making the process quick and efficient. He helped the SEO teams grow new users by <span style="color: #E3120B; font-weight: bold; background: #fff0ef; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">100x from non-brand SEO</span>, and was part of the initial leadership of the Growth team established under Chamath Palihapitiya in 2007.

## 3. Guiding Principle #1: Tools Evolve, But Principles Are Timeless

***"No matter the technology, it's always still marketing."***

Schultz's first guiding principle addresses a common trap: getting lost in the complexity of new technology rather than recognizing the <span style="color: #006BA2; font-weight: bold; background: #f0f8ff; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">timeless marketing principles</span> that make any tool effective. As a physics student entering the marketing field, he had to learn these principles from scratch, relying on resources like <span style="color: #9A607F; font-weight: bold; background: #f5f0f3; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">Ogilvy on Advertising</span>, which provided advice applicable to every new endeavor regardless of when or with what tools.

The author illustrates this principle through the evolution of <span style="color: #3EBCD2; font-weight: bold; background: #e0f7fa; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">direct marketing channels</span>. When push notifications for mobile apps became popular, many companies got overwhelmed by technical questions: How do you build an app? How do you prompt for push permission? How do you register a push token? How do you send a notification? They focused on the technology rather than stepping back to consider the fundamental marketing principles that would make the tool effective.

Across all these channels‚Äîpostal mail, email, SMS, and push notifications‚Äîthe principles remained constant:

- **Delivery:** You need accurate, current contact information
- **Compelling content:** The message must motivate the recipient to open and engage
- **Measurement:** You must justify the incremental benefit of sending the message
- **Spam risk:** Being marked as unwanted can permanently damage your ability to reach that person

This principle extends beyond direct marketing to most online marketing work. The technology may change dramatically, but the underlying marketing principles‚Äîunderstanding your audience, crafting compelling messages, measuring results, optimizing based on data‚Äîremain constant. Marketers who grasp this can avoid being paralyzed by new technology and instead focus on applying proven principles through new channels.

## 4. Guiding Principle #2: Incremental Results Are Everything

***"An incremental result is one that you can prove happened because of your actions. In other words, if you hadn't done the marketing, would anything have happened?"***

The second guiding principle addresses what Schultz considers the most critical question in marketing: <span style="color: #E3120B; font-weight: bold; background: #fff0ef; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">did your marketing actually cause the outcome you're measuring?</span> Ideally, the answer is that nothing would have happened without your marketing. But in reality, when marketers appropriately self-scrutinize, they usually discover that some results would have occurred anyway. The real question becomes: <span style="color: #006BA2; font-weight: bold; background: #f0f8ff; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">how much less would have happened without the marketing?</span>

<div style="background: linear-gradient(135deg, #fff0ef 0%, #ffebee 100%); border-left: 4px solid #E3120B; border-radius: 8px; padding: 14px; margin: 14px 0; box-shadow: 0 2px 8px rgba(227,18,11,0.15);">
  <div style="font-weight: bold; color: #A81829; margin-bottom: 6px; font-size: 1.05em;">‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Warning</div>
  <div style="color: #333;">The difference between tracking what happens after someone clicks your ad versus running proper experiments to see what impact your ad created can mean the difference between wasting millions of dollars and driving genuine business growth.</div>
</div>

### The eBay Brand Search Case Study

The most canonical example of this principle comes from <span style="color: #379A8B; font-weight: bold; background: #e8f5f3; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">Professor Steve Tadelis's research at eBay</span>, conducted years after Schultz left the company. eBay had been buying the keyword <span style="color: #3EBCD2; font-weight: bold; background: #e0f7fa; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">'eBay'</span> so that ads for the marketplace appeared when users searched for the word. The company's marketing team believed these ads were driving valuable user acquisitions.

Tadelis hypothesized that users who clicked on these ads <span style="color: #E3120B; font-weight: bold; background: #fff0ef; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">would have converted without eBay buying the ad</span>‚Äîthey were already searching for eBay specifically, after all. He ran experiments that proved the vast majority of users eBay's internet marketing team thought were coming from the search term 'eBay' would have come to and used the platform anyway. The company was essentially <span style="color: #E3120B; font-weight: bold; background: #fff0ef; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">paying to acquire users who would have converted regardless</span>‚Äîwasting money on ads that were doing next to nothing for them.

<div style="background: linear-gradient(135deg, #e8f5f3 0%, #c8e6c9 100%); border-left: 4px solid #379A8B; border-radius: 8px; padding: 14px; margin: 14px 0; box-shadow: 0 2px 8px rgba(55,154,139,0.15);">
  <div style="font-weight: bold; color: #005F52; margin-bottom: 6px; font-size: 1.05em;">‚úÖ Success Factor</div>
  <div style="color: #333;">By running proper experiments with holdout groups, eBay discovered they could eliminate spending on brand search terms and redirect those resources to channels that actually drove incremental growth‚Äîsaving millions while improving overall marketing efficiency.</div>
</div>

### The Uber Fraud Case Study

Fraud provides another compelling reason why tracking incremental results is essential. Schultz's favorite example involves <span style="color: #379A8B; font-weight: bold; background: #e8f5f3; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">Uber during the late-2010s land grab</span> for ride booking apps. The company spent <span style="color: #E3120B; font-weight: bold; background: #fff0ef; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">hundreds of millions of dollars</span> on ads that supposedly drove their growth.

<div style="display: inline-block; border: 2px solid #E3120B; border-radius: 8px; padding: 10px 14px; margin: 6px 3px; background: linear-gradient(135deg, #fff0ef 0%, #ffebee 100%); box-shadow: 0 2px 8px rgba(227,18,11,0.1);">
  <div style="font-size: 1.8em; font-weight: bold; color: #E3120B; line-height: 1;">$100M+</div>
  <div style="color: #758D99; font-size: 0.85em; margin-top: 2px;">Wasted on Fraudulent Ads</div>
</div>

In 2017, Uber's CMO <span style="color: #006BA2; font-weight: bold; background: #f0f8ff; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">Kevin Frisch</span> discovered something shocking: when he turned off some ads that supposedly drove growth, <span style="color: #E3120B; font-weight: bold; background: #fff0ef; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">there was no actual drop in growth</span>. Digging deeper into the data, he realized that a group of companies was using Uber's tracking improperly, taking credit for user registrations and defrauding Uber of millions in the process. Uber won the resulting court case and saved the company <span style="color: #379A8B; font-weight: bold; background: #e8f5f3; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">hundreds of millions of dollars</span> over subsequent years.

<div style="border-left: 5px solid #006BA2; background: linear-gradient(90deg, #f0f8ff 0%, #ffffff 100%); padding: 20px; margin: 20px 0; border-radius: 0 8px 8px 0; box-shadow: 0 2px 8px rgba(0,107,162,0.1);">
  <div style="font-style: italic; font-size: 1.15em; color: #006BA2; font-weight: 600; line-height: 1.6; margin-bottom: 8px;">"Half the money I spend on advertising is wasted; the trouble is I don't know which half."</div>

Schultz argues that Wanamaker's famous quote actually understates the problem. In some instances, it's <span style="color: #E3120B; font-weight: bold; background: #fff0ef; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">the entirety of a marketing channel</span> that gets wasted, not just half. The tools have changed from offline to digital marketing, but the need for measuring incremental returns has remained constant.

### The Solution: Testing and Experimentation

The solution to this problem mirrors Schultz's approach to running GeoCities websites in the 1990s and 2000s: <span style="color: #9A607F; font-weight: bold; background: #f5f0f3; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">systematic testing</span>. These tests can range from sophisticated multivariate experiments using advanced statistics to simple before-and-after comparisons. The complexity required depends on your business context:

## 5. The Author's Journey: From GeoCities to Meta CMO

***"My whole life I have enjoyed online marketing and analytics, and I see them as two sides of the same coin."***

Schultz's credibility stems from an unusual career trajectory that began not in business school or advertising agencies, but with <span style="color: #379A8B; font-weight: bold; background: #e8f5f3; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">personal necessity and genuine passion</span>. His parents provided an excellent education, sending him to a great private school through sacrifices. By the end of high school, however, the family's financial situation had tightened. Schultz needed a scholarship to continue at school (which he won) and gained a needs-tested bursary to attend <span style="color: #006BA2; font-weight: bold; background: #f0f8ff; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">Cambridge University</span>.

He took his small <span style="color: #3EBCD2; font-weight: bold; background: #e0f7fa; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">GeoCities website about paper airplanes</span> and transformed it from a hobby into a real income generator.

### The GeoCities Breakthrough

In the 1990s, GeoCities was one of the few hosting services where you could set up your own website‚Äîa somewhat Wild West precursor to today's standardized, sleek sites. Schultz's site surprisingly started getting visitors from search engines, primarily <span style="color: #3EBCD2; font-weight: bold; background: #e0f7fa; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">AltaVista</span> at the time, all on his paper airplanes section. The traffic came because someone had added a link to his site from their far more popular website.

This was a revelation: <span style="color: #E3120B; font-weight: bold; background: #fff0ef; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">the link was more valuable for the search ranking power it gave him than for the direct visitors clicking on it</span>. Schultz wanted more visitors, so over time he learned <span style="color: #9A607F; font-weight: bold; background: #f5f0f3; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">search engine optimization (SEO)</span>. His experience exposed him to SEO's power, and he was able to use it to rank first in Google for <span style="color: #379A8B; font-weight: bold; background: #e8f5f3; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 3px;">'Paper Airplanes'</span>.

- Affiliate marketers taking credit for conversions that would have happened without them
